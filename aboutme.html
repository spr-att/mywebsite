About Me

I am a recent graduate from the College of William and Mary (W&M) with a B.S. in Biology, a minor in Data Science, and a particular interest in programming and conservation. My journey into the world of Data Science began my sophomore year at W&M, when I started researching with my ecology professor, Matthias Leu. The project I joined observed the factors that influence occupancy patterns of frogs in Eastern Virginia by using Microsoft Excel and modeling in R. After working on the project I realized that I had a real passion for programming and wanted to learn other languages as well as improve my coding skills in general.

To do this, I decided to pursue a minor in Data Science, which was one of the best decisions I made in my college career. The initial courses I took for my minor gave me an introduction into object-oriented programming with Python which has now become the language that I am the most experienced in. I also decided to take some GIS classes which showed me the importance of data visualization. To further my experience with all these software I have completed projects that allowed me to grow my skills beyond the classroom.
